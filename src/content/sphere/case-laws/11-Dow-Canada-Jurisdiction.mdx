## Extended Background

Global chemical leaders like Dow have layered supply chains and financing across geographies. In Canada, Dow sought to challenge a downward TP adjustment, prompting debate over whether the Tax Court or Federal Court had jurisdiction for such administrative matters.

## Detailed Arguments

### Taxpayer

- Claimed that all TP cases, including challenges to Ministerial discretion, belong in the Tax Court for efficient resolution.

### Revenue

- Maintained that only the Federal Court could properly consider exercise-of-discretion issues.

### Court Reasoning

- The Supreme Court of Canada ruled that while the Tax Court handles substantive TP disputes, discretionary decisions about assessment only belong in the Federal Court.

## Procedural Journey

- Started with Tax Court challenges, but the issue was elevated to the Supreme Court for national clarification.

- Ruled in favor of the Crown, setting a procedural framework for future challenges.

## Implications Beyond the Case

- Highlights the importance of choosing the right forum for each dispute—crucial for procedural success.

- For MNEs: consult counsel and file in the correct court to avoid costly, time-consuming missteps.
